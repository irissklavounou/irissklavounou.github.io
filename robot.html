Product Development: "The Litter Robots"

The Litter Robots project aimed to address New York's waste management issue, with a particular focus on creating a viable solution. The team followed a structured approach, beginning with a compelling pitch akin to Shark Tank. Despite the challenges posed by the remote COVID environment, significant progress was made during the three-week timeframe. To establish feasibility, a Minimal Viable Product (MVP) was brainstormed and tested. In parallel, the team employed the Project Management software Miro to develop a comprehensive Business Model Canvas, encompassing the target customer, value proposition, revenue model, channels, and customer relationships. Validation of hypotheses was achieved through a series of more than five interviews conducted during the idea development phase. Throughout the project, crucial metrics were employed for decision making and compromise, utilizing tools like decision matrices and Planning Poker. Iterative refinement of the epic statement, sub-statement, and business model canvas occurred as a result of insights gained from interviews and tests, aligning the project with evolving customer needs and market demands.

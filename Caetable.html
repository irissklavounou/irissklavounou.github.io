Executive Stone Desk Design - Computer Aided Engineering Analysis

This project aimed to design an executive stone-topped desk with dimensions and materials capable of withstanding specified load parameters. ANSYS APDL was used to ensure deformation and stress resistance, adhering to design constraints under regular usage. Iterations were made to optimize table legs, crossbeams, material, and support plate thickness. The final design featured Stainless Steel 304 square beams (0.08m x 0.08m) for legs and crossbeams, an Aluminum 6061 support plate (0.03m thickness) under an 18mm granite tabletop. The design met stress (FOS: 14) and buckling (FOS: 4.85) safety factors, withstanding its own weight and a 1112N point load on the table surface.

In modeling the table with ANSYS, simplifications were made for efficiency and to avoid singularities or truncation errors. The table deflection was constrained diagonally, representing a worst-case loading scenario. Legs and perimeter framing were modeled as 1D beams with a square cross-section to reduce computational resources. The tabletop and support plate were assumed to be glued, modeled as a 2D shell with aluminum and granite layers, and attached to the perimeter and diagonal beams. This assumption is reasonable, as construction would likely involve screws or adhesive, not welding.

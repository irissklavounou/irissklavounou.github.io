Assessing John Locke's depiction of the state of nature in the second treatise of government

In the Second Treatise, "civil society" is shown to be derived from the "state of nature" that mankind is born into and that is governed by the law of nature. This law dictates that all men are equal, and so by being equal, each individial is his own executioner of the right to preserve mankind. Locke uses this principle of equality to also introduce justice, whereby the victim is given the right to punish the offender and reciprocate the offence. These two attributes, equality and justice, are the main defining characteristics of Locke's depiction of the state of nature.

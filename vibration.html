VIBRATION PROJECT: MODELING COOPER HYPERLOOP'S HORIZONTAL STABILITY.

To enhance the efficiency and safety of Hyperloop pod operations, our team constructed a scaled testing rig to analyze and mitigate disruptions caused by bumps along the I-beam track. The rig, representing a 2-degree of freedom system, emulates an uneven track and utilizes a trailing arm suspension, stepper motor, and Arduino microcontroller. Through a Scotch yoke linkage mechanism, the motor generates oscillating vertical motion, resulting in compression and decompression of a linkage arm connected to the top of the system. This enables the evaluation of spring dynamics and facilitates the gathering of empirical data on displacement and performance using Tracker software.

As seen by the gif below, the right linear rail is not as smooth given that ball bearings were falling out, causing extra friction.These were cheap linear rails sourced from Amazon. The rest is laser cut wood, metal cut iron for the mass, and a 3D printed knob.
